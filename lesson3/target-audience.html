Target Audience and Personas

The target audience of Weather Vane 2 Go is described below:
Who: Adults, children, visitors, and families who are interested in weather forecasts
Age: 10-100
Technology: They own a computer, tablet or smart phone and possess basic internet navigation skills
Income: Income levels will typically vary between no income and $60,000 annually
Motivation: Improve safety and enjoyment of a trips and daily activities with rooster humor.

John Persona

Occupation: Student
Demographics and Education: 12 years old attending 7th grade at Preston Middle School. Oldest of 4.
Delivers newspapers, mows lawns and shovels snow.
Goals and motivation for using the site: Learn what to wear for school and appropriate weather for outdoor jobs.
Social: Very active individual, loves being outdoors with siblings and friends. Enjoys hikes, camping and collecting rocks.
Technology: Uses Kindle Fire. Parents haven’t bought him his own phone yet, but he’s working on it. He is saving up for one. He doesn’t get great cell reception everyplace in town, so he isn’t in too much of a hurry. Uses Microsoft Office for school and also knows how to search the internet for school projects.
Quote: “I can’t wait till I am old enough to drive.”

Mary Persona

Occupation: Grocery Store Cashier/Part-time Daycare/Seasonal Ranger
Demographics and Education: 26 years old. High school diploma, one semester of Jr. College. Grew up in Preston on a chicken farm. Her fiancé is a chicken farmer.
Goals and motivation for using the site: Learn what to wear for work and appropriate weather and road conditions for drives to Yellowstone.
Social: Very active individual, loves being outdoors. Loves to take road trips to explore new places. Enjoys hikes, camping, canoeing and rock climbing.
Technology: Uses Dell laptop purchased online and smartphone. Has online accounts for work, insurance, banking, and social media. Checks social media daily to keep in touch with friends and family. Knows Microsoft office, Excel and PowerPoint.
Quote: “I’d rather be outside. There’s always time for one more adventure.”

Scenarios

  1. What should I wear to school (or work) today?
  2. What are the road conditions like in Preston (or other nearby cities)?
  3. What gear should I plan to bring on my hike?
  4. Is it a good idea to go camping this weekend?
  5. Will I need to give the chickens extra water or protection from a storm?
